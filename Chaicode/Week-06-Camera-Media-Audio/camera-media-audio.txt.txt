

expo camera
exposes the device camera through a React Native component called
CameraView
--> it wraps native camera APIs on iOS and Andriod (and limited web support) so you can :
 -- Show a live camera preview in your app
 -- Take photos and record videos
 -- Scan barcodes (QR, EAN,Code 128 etc)  

1. Camera Preview --- live video feed renders on react tree (native camera session).
   its not a static image its a native camera session bound to a view
2. Camera Session --- the native  resource that owns camera hardware. starting a preview
   opens a session, unmounting CameraView should close it.

Facing (Camera Type)
  -- back - rear camera default
  -- front - selfie camera

Mode (Camera Mode)
  -- picture  - still photo capture
  -- video   --- movie recording


Flash (Flash Mode)
Burst of light at the moment a photo is taken : 0ff  | on | auto

Torch 
LED Kept on continuously during preview, Controlled with enableTorch. Separate from flash


Zoom
Normalized number 0 to 1 (0= widest, 1=max zoom on device). not the same as 
"2x optical zoom".

Capture URI
local file path(or base64 on web) written  on app cache. Copy to permanent storage
if user need file long-term.


Barcode Scanning
Enabled with barcodeScannerSettings + onBarcodeScanned. The callback may fire 
repeatedly while a code stays in frame -- dedupe in your app.
